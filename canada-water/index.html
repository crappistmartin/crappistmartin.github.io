Canada Water

Jul 24th, 2015 2:55 pm

Canada Water Site A

This first phase of the Canada Water regeneration comprised 9 buildings ranging up to 26 storeys, providing 668 residential units of which 47 are shared ownership and 123 are ‘affordable rent’ of 80% market rent.

The developer was barratts, which has branded the development ‘Maple Quays’ and the housing association is Affinity Sutton

The planning case report for the planning application behind this development, informed the general public and committee members that it would provide 123 social rented homes:

Officer report for Site A planning permission

However, when the section 106 legal agreement was drawn up, there was no mention of social rented housing - only ‘affordable rent’ (i.e. 80% market rent):

Canada Water scheme very popular, especially with councillors. Colley bought two of the new properties off plan.

LR sales agreement for the site. Orignally CPO’d by Swk in the 1970s, then taken over by LDDC, now being flogged off for a pittance for luxury housing.

NHHT bought land for £50m - much more than the £16m paid for it in … (/images/LR_CanadaWater.pdf)

Officers stood firm on original application insisting that the site could support 28% affordable housing at the policy compliant tenure mix (shared ownership/social rented).

GLA report says that the previous application (approved by planning committee in 2010) for this site was for 430 homes of which 74 would be social rented. But this wasn’t implemented. http://planbuild.southwark.gov.uk/documents/?casereference=09/AP/1783&system=DC (/images/CanadaWaterPreviousMix.png)

Instead we get a revised application for 1030 homes of which just 34 were supposed to be social rented. Now that NHHT is involved, there are twice as many homes and half the number of social rented. Whatsmore it seems that these are now going to be affordable rent instead.
