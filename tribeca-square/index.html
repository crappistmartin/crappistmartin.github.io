Tribeca Square

Jan 18th, 2015 1:38 pm

This site used to be a council-owned industrial estate, volvo garage and small SMEs.

Originaly bought by EADON (Isle of Man) - Oakmayne?

Then bought by Delancey DV4 for £16m (but LR registry says £40m)?

Original permission was granted in 2006.

In December 2008, the developers came back to planning committee asking for a variation in its s106 agreement.

Despite adding 61 extra private residential units to the revised scheme, the developer argued that because of the economic downturn the scheme was no longer viable enough to provide any affordable housing onsite or any payment in-lieu of affordable housing.

The planning officer’s report recommending approval of the development, stated that the council’s in-house valuers had confirmed that “the negative effect of on-site social rented housing on the end values of the private units could in this case render the scheme financially unviable”. The report went on to explain that the design implications of providing a separate ‘poor door’ for social housing “wouldn’t support the planning objective of creating a strong and continuous retail frontage at the base of the towers.”

Under Southwark’s tariff for in-lieu affordable housing payments this development should have provided a total of £35m to the council. Instead the council got nothing because the viability assessment submitted by developers said the development wasn’t viable.

As with any major planning application, developers need to show that they have consulted on their plans with the local community. The developer in this case chose to instruct Four Communications the communications company run by Southwark’s former council leader to conduct its community consultation exercise; during which community support for the application was evidenced in a questionnaire and graph showing broad support from a total of 5 local people.

We managed to get our hands on a copy of this viability assessment - £534 per square foot etc..
