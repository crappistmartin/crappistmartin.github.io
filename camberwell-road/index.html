Camberwell Road

Feb 28th, 2016 1:00 pm

Camberwell Road/Wyndham Road

With the redevelopment of the Aylesbury and Elmington estates regeneration mania has now spread to Camberwell.

This is announce at the top end of Camberwell road, where a major development comprising new retail space and 84 residential units. The planning report for which confirms that of the 84 new homes just 10 will be social rented. The development will also include a number of art studios for the Artists Studio Company, which is a development partner in the deal.

A number of period buildings and shop frontages on Camberwell road were demolished to make way for this development.

240 Camberwell Road

Further down the road is 240 Camberwell Road, which used to be home to several warehouses functioning as the operating headquarters of Scena Ltd - a joinery manufacturer which has been forced to relocate to Croydon.

240 Camberwell Road before demolition

The site was also home to a drop-in centre for Bleheim CDP a charity providing support for people with drug and alcohol addiction problems.

The planning report for the application confirms that the community space occupied by the charity will not be re-provided in the redevelopment.

It also confirms that of the 164 new homes on the site, just 18 will be social rented (11%).

Peabody’s Camberwell Rd development

Further down the road still, Peabody has demolished a number of buildings and shop fronts adjacent to the Bingo Hall. It is currently building 66 new homes on this site. It’s planning application for the redevelopment confirms that it submitted a viability assessment and as a result can only viably provide 14 social rented homes.

This was agreed by the Council and the development is currently under constructiion.

http://www.peabody.org.uk/homes-in-development/southwark/camberwell-road

Camberwell on the Green

As we reach Camberwell Green we are confronted with a development ingeniously branded ‘Camberwell on the Green’.

This development is being built on the site of the former Job Centre. It comprises 1,335 sqm of new shop frontage along with 101 new residential units. The developer submitted a customary viability assessment claiming poverty and successfully argued that the development could not support any social rented housing. Instead it will provide just 16 shared ownership units as the entirety of its affordable housing offer.

The planning report for the application (submitted in 2012) said that there would be a review mechanism secured in the section 106 agreement to ensure that the affordable housing offer would be increased in line with any increase in property market values.(para 131)

However, the planning control documents show that there has been no such viability review and no corresponding change in this development’s pitiful affordable housing offer.(12/AP/1308)
